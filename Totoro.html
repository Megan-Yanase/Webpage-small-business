Totoro Mini Calendar Tutorial:

Materials Needed:

  • Air dry clay (gray, white, black, and any other colors you prefer)
  • Rolling pin
  • Craft knife or clay sculpting tools
  • Toothpick
  • Paint and brushes (optional)
  • Dice molds or small cube shapes for dates
  • Rectangular mold or shape for the month base

Instructions:

Prepare the Clay:

Start by kneading the gray air dry clay until it is soft and pliable. This will be used for Totoro’s body.

Create Totoro’s Body:

  1. Roll the gray clay into an oval shape to form Totoro’s body.
  2. Flatten the bottom slightly so it can stand upright.

Make Totoro’s Belly:

  1. Use white clay to create Totoro’s belly. Roll it into a small oval and flatten it.
  2. Attach the white belly to the front of the gray body by gently pressing it on.

Add Totoro’s Features:

  1. Use small pieces of black clay to create Totoro’s eyes, nose, and mouth. Roll tiny balls for the eyes and press them onto the face. Add even smaller white clay balls inside the black eyes.
  2. For the nose and mouth, use the black clay to form a small nose and a simple smile.
  3. Use a toothpick to create the whiskers by lightly etching them on either side of the nose.

Create Totoro’s Ears and Arms:

  1. Shape two small cones from the gray clay for the ears and attach them to the top of Totoro’s head.
  2. For the arms, roll two small cylinders from the gray clay and attach them to the sides of Totoro’s body.

Make the Dice for the Dates:

  1. Use white or gray clay to form two small cubes, or use dice molds if you have them.
  2. Smooth out the edges and ensure they are even.
  3. Use a toothpick or sculpting tool to carve the numbers 1 through 6 on each cube. Ensure that the numbers are carved deeply enough to be visible after the clay dries.
  4. Optional: Paint the numbers in a contrasting color for better visibility.

Create the Month Base:

  1. Roll out a piece of clay (color of your choice) into a flat rectangular shape.
  2. Use a craft knife to cut out a rectangle approximately 1.5 inches by 4 inches.
  3. Smooth out the edges and ensure the surface is even.
  4. Use the toothpick to carve the names of the months into the rectangle, or use paint to write them after the clay dries.

Assemble the Calendar:

  1. Place the dice in front of Totoro so they are visible.
  2. Stand the rectangular month base behind Totoro or lean it against a support to keep it upright.
  3. Let all the clay pieces dry according to the manufacturer’s instructions, typically 24-48 hours.

Optional Finishing Touches:

Once all pieces are dry, you can paint any additional details or add a coat of clear varnish to protect the clay and give it a glossy finish.
